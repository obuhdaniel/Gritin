# Goliard Brand Identity Guide

## Brand Overview

**Goliard** (from goliards — medieval wandering scholars who wrote of love, wine, and adventure) is a lifestyle platform for women in graduate school navigating the beautiful contradiction of being deeply intellectual AND deeply alive: in love, in motion, in discovery.

## Core Positioning

> "You can be brilliant AND unbothered. Ambitious AND in love. Graduating AND going somewhere wild."

## Target Audience

- **Age**: Women, 22–35
- **Education**: In Masters/PhD programs
- **Heritage**: Pan-African, Black, global South diaspora
- **Interests**: Travelers, nature lovers, hodophiles (people who love travel for the sake of it)
- **Relationships**: In relationships or dating; emotionally intelligent
- **Lifestyle**: Urban and internationally mobile
- **Tech**: Tech-comfortable but craving authenticity

## Brand Personality

- **Warm intelligence** — not cold academia, not shallow lifestyle
- **Wanderlust with roots** — African heritage + global curiosity
- **Unapologetic duality** — she studies AND she lives
- **Soft power** — feminine, powerful, not loud about it

## Typography

### Display: Cormorant Garamond

Elegant serif with soul — used for headlines, brand name, and quotes.

```css
font-family: var(--font-cormorant);
```

**Weights**: 300, 400, 500, 600, 700

### Body: DM Sans

Humanist sans-serif for readability — used for body text, UI elements.

```css
font-family: var(--font-dm-sans);
```

**Weights**: 400, 500, 600, 700

### Accent

Italic serif for quotes and special emphasis.

## Tone of Voice

### In Communications

- **Warm**: Write like a wise older sister who studied abroad
- **Empowering**: Don't tell her what she can't do
- **Honest**: Acknowledge the contradictions she feels
- **Inviting**: Use "we" and "she" to create community

### Messaging Examples

**Do**: "She's building something. Are you in?"
**Don't**: "Sign up now for exclusive content!"

**Do**: "The woman who reads theory by day and dances by night."
**Don't**: "The ultimate platform for grad students."

## Visual Guidelines

### Aesthetic

Warm editorial maximalism — think ELLE Africa meets NYT Magazine meets travel diary.

### Prohibited

- ❌ Purple gradients
- ❌ Generic SaaS aesthetic
- ❌ Stock-photo language
- ❌ Cold, clinical design

### Required

- ✅ Warm, textured backgrounds
- ✅ Organic shapes and curves
- ✅ Natural color transitions
- ✅ Human-centered photography
- ✅ Emotive, resonant headlines

## Page Sections

### 1. Hero
- Full-viewport height
- Bold serif headline, 2–3 lines
- Subheadline naming the tension
- Warm textured background with subtle animation
- CTA: "Join the Waitlist"

### 2. The Tension Section
Name what she feels — the guilt of wanting fun, the loneliness of being "too ambitious" or "too free".
Example: "Nobody told you that getting your Masters would also mean explaining why you still want to dance, travel, fall in love, and stay out late."

### 3. Features / Pillars
- The World Map: Travel for budget grad students — hidden gems, student discounts, visa tips
- The Reading Room: Academic wellness, thesis support, burnout guides
- Love & Latitude: Relationships while being mobile, ambitious, and emotionally complex
- The Nature Diaries: Slow travel, ecotourism, nature retreats
- The Pan-African Thread: Afrodiaspora perspectives on education, identity, belonging

### 4. Community
3 fictional member portraits — Name, city, degree, dream destination (Lagos, Accra, London, Atlanta, Paris)

### 5. Manifesto
Second-person letter "Dear Goliard," — names her contradictions with love. Tone: wise older sister who studied abroad and fell in love in Lisbon.

### 6. Waitlist CTA
- Headline: "She's building something. Are you in?"
- Email input + button
- Social proof placeholder

### 7. Footer
Logo, links, social handles
